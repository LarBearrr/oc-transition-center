partner in giving someone a second chance

Employment is essential forincarcerated individuals to return to society.

Jobs help with rehabilitation and create opportunities to start a new life.

Why hire someone who was formerly incarcerated?

Many times, these individuals have the knowledge, skills, and abilities needed to fulfil the position and duties you are looking for.

Dependable

In general, former offenders are as dependable as other employees (United States Probation Office, Eastern District of Wisconsin).

Accountable

Offenders are monitored through records checks, office and field contacts, and drug testing to ensure that they comply with the conditions of our program.

Available

Our clients are available to begin work within a week of arriving to our facility. They have access to public and transportation.

Additional Employer Incentives

As an employer, there are additional incentives available to you for working with an offender. These programs help save you time and money, at no cost to you!

Save Money

All of our candidates are eligibe for the Work Opportunity Tax Credit. Your business can apply to earn a $2,400 tax credit contingent on an offer of employment.

Minimize Risk

Each employee is bondable and insurable, at no cost to you, through the Federal Bonding Program, for up to $5,000, renewable every 6 months for 3 years. Our bonding agent will handle all of the paperwork for you.

Certified Candidates

Many of our cadidates have received their Kansas WORKReady! Certificate through the Workforce Centers of Southcentral KAnsas. This certifies the worker has the skills you need to fill your open position(s).
