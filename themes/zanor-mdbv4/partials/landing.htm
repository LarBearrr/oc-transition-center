at Wichita Work Release

Work Release strives to provide inmates with an opportunity to gain and maintain full-time employment; save money to be used upon parole release; pay dependent support, if applicable; pay any restitution and court costs owed; and, in general, learn to manage their money and lives under the supervision of employers and facility staff.

Why us?

New beginnings

Employment is essential for previously incarcerated individuals returning to society. Jobs help with rehabilitation and create opportunities to start a new life.

Work ready

Many times, these individuals have the knowledge, skills, and abilities needed to fulfill the position and duties you are looking for.

Employer incentives

As an employer, there are additional incentives available to you for working with an offender. These programs help save you time and money, at no cost to you!

Available

Our clients are ready to start work within a week of their arrival to our program. Most are applying to jobs within 72 hours of their arrival.

Accountable

Offenders are monitored through records checks, office and field contacts, and drug testing to ensure that they comply with the conditions of our program.

Dependable

In general, former offenders are as dependable as other employees (United States Probation Office, Eastern District of Wisconsin).

Want more?

As an employer, you are eligible for a number of incentive programs to help save your business money, minimize risk, and pay for training.

SAVE MONEY

All of our candidates are eligible for the Work Opportunity Tax Credit. Your business can apply to earn a $2,400 tax credit contingent on an offer of employment.

Minimize Risk

Each employee is bondable and insurable, at no cost to you, through the Federal Bonding Program, for up to $5,000, renewable every 6 months for 3 years. Our bonding agent will handle all of the paperwork for you.

Grant Programs

Gain access to the Workforce Innovation Fund. Funds are available for On-the-Job Training (OJT) to employers who hire eligible participants. Our program participants qualify for this grant.

Community Partners

We are currently partnered with over 130 employers in a dozen different industries, from dishwashers to draftsmen, and entry level to electricians.
